About the Medea Story

Medea is a tragedy by the ancient Greek playwright Euripides based on a myth. It was first performed in 431 BC as part of a trilogy, the other plays of which have not survived. Its plot centers on the actions of Medea, a former princess of the kingdom of Colchis and the wife of Jason; she finds her position in the world threatened as Jason leaves her for a princess of Corinth and takes vengeance on him by murdering his new wife and her own two sons, before escaping to Athens to start a new life.

Main Characters:

Medea is a powerful witch, daughter of Aeetes, king of Colchis, and granddaughter of Helios, the sun god. She fell in love with Jason and helped him to steal the Golden Fleece. Medea betrayed her father, killed her brother, and killed Jason's uncle, all to help Jason to get his throne.

Jason is Medea's husband and the leader of the Argonauts. He wants to achieve his own goals. He leaves Medea to marry Glauce, the daughter of Creon, the King of Corinth, hoping to gain more power and a better future for himself.

The Nurse is a loyal servant of Medea, the Nurse cares for Medea's children and says her concerns when Medea decides to kill her children.

Tutor of Medea and Jason's sons a teacher and caretaker for Medea and Jason's children. The Tutor is a practical and observant character who helps look after the kids and sometimes comments on the events unfolding around them.

Creon is the King of Corinth and father of Glauce. He fears Medea's potential for revenge and orders her exile, but gets killed at the end.

Aegeus is the King of Athens who promises to help Medea by giving her a safe place to live after she is forced to leave Corinth.
